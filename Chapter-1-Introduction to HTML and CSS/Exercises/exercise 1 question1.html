Hyderabadi Biryani

Hyderabadi Biryani is a rich and flavorful rice dish that originated in Hyderabad, India. It is made by layering basmati rice with marinated meat, aromatic spices, and fried onions, then cooked together to perfection. It's a festive dish often served during special occasions.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups basmati rice
  • 500g chicken or mutton, cleaned
  • 1 cup yogurt
  • 2 onions, thinly sliced and fried
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons ginger-garlic paste
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1/2 teaspoon saffron soaked in 1/4 cup warm milk
  • Fresh coriander and mint leaves
  • Whole spices (bay leaves, cloves, cinnamon, cardamom)
  • Salt to taste
  • 3 tablespoons oil or ghee

Method

  1. Wash and soak the basmati rice for 30 minutes. Boil until 70% cooked and drain.
  2. Marinate the meat with yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, turmeric, red chili powder, and salt. Let it sit for at least 1 hour.
  3. Heat oil or ghee in a large pot. Add whole spices and sauté for a minute.
  4. Add chopped tomatoes and cook until soft.
  5. Add the marinated meat and cook until it is almost done.
  6. Layer half of the cooked rice over the meat. Add some fried onions, mint, and coriander leaves.
  7. Add the remaining rice, more fried onions, and the saffron milk on top.
  8. Cover the pot with a tight lid and cook on low heat (dum) for 20–25 minutes.
  9. Gently mix before serving. Serve hot with raita or salad.
